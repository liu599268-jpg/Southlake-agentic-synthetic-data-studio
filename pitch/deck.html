Cautions — 3/3
Planning use boundary

What the Studio Is Safe For,
and What It Is Not

Synthetic data is powerful for planning and experimentation. But it is not local operational truth, and it should not be used without governance and validation.

Good For

Planning workshops and innovation sprints — test routing, capacity, and community-diversion ideas safely
Scenario-driven discussions about Southlake's distributed-campus future state
Prototyping dashboards, workflows, and analytics without real patient records
Pitch-day storytelling backed by visible metrics, AI reasoning, and exportable artifacts

Not Ready For

Clinical care, staffing commitments, reimbursement, or patient-level action
Claims about Southlake's real patient mix from a U.S. public-use source
A full hospital digital twin or validated multi-table operational model
Production use without governance, privacy review, and validation on governed local data
Best Next Step

Validate this workflow with governed Southlake data, operational owners, and formal privacy review. The agentic architecture is ready — it needs real local data to become a real planning tool.

Why This Slide Matters

In healthcare, the team that says "here's what we don't know yet" is more trustworthy than the team that claims everything is solved. This caution boundary is a feature, not a weakness.
