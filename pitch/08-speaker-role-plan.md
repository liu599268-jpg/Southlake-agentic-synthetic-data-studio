# Speaker Role Plan

Use this file to assign actual team members to speaking slots before pitch day.

## Recommended 3-Presenter Split

Replace `Presenter A`, `Presenter B`, and `Presenter C` with real names.

### Presenter A

- Own the opening problem statement.
- Explain why Southlake specifically needs a safer experimentation workflow.
- Walk Slide 1: methodology and architecture.
- Deliver the closing argument and final call to action.

### Presenter B

- Drive the live app demo.
- Open the studio at `http://127.0.0.1:3100`.
- Click `Load recommended demo run`.
- Walk the saved `Distributed Campus Routing` output.
- Walk Slide 2: product features and saved-run reliability.

### Presenter C

- Own Slide 3: cautions and governance boundary.
- Lead the discussion of why the source is public and why that is acceptable for a workflow prototype.
- Handle privacy, governance, and limitation questions during judge Q and A.

## Recommended 2-Presenter Fallback

If only two people speak:

### Presenter A

- Opening
- Why Southlake
- Slide 1 methodology
- Closing

### Presenter B

- Full live demo
- Slide 2 features
- Slide 3 cautions
- Most of the Q and A

## Hand-Off Lines

Use these exact transitions if the team wants cleaner hand-offs.

### A -> B

"We have framed the problem and the workflow. Now we will show the studio itself and how a Southlake-aligned planning run appears on screen."

### B -> C

"We also want to be explicit about what this tool is not. I will hand over to cover the caution boundary and what healthcare professionals should be careful about."

### C -> A

"Those limitations are why we position this as a safer front-end to planning, not a final decision system. I will hand back for the close."

## Role Selection Rules

- The strongest speaker should take the opening and closing.
- The most technically confident speaker should run the demo.
- The most careful, credible explainer should handle limitations and Q and A.
- Do not switch control between speakers during the live click path unless the team has rehearsed it multiple times.

## Final Step Before Pitch Day

- Replace role labels with actual names.
- Print or save one copy for the whole team.
- Rehearse once with the full hand-off script and once without it.
