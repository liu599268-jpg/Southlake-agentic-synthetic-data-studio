# Judge Q and A

## Q1. Why use synthetic data at all?

Use this answer:

"Because healthcare teams need a safer way to test planning ideas early. Synthetic data creates a sandbox for experimentation without directly using real patient records."

## Q2. Why do you call this agentic?

Use this answer:

"Because the system does more than generate data. It frames the goal, profiles the dataset, chooses a generation plan, evaluates the output, and writes caution guidance."

## Q3. Why use a U.S. public-use dataset instead of Ontario data?

Use this answer:

"For the hackathon, we wanted a safe, public, and reproducible source. The goal here is to prove the workflow and product pattern, not to claim we already have Southlake-grade local validity."

## Q4. Is synthetic data automatically private?

Use this answer:

"No. It is not automatically private. That is why we score overlap risk, show caution notes, and treat this as a planning prototype rather than a production privacy guarantee."

## Q5. Why is this relevant to Southlake specifically?

Use this answer:

"Southlake's challenge is about a distributed health network. That means needing better ways to model routing, capacity, observation, and community-based care pathways. Our product is built around exactly those scenario questions."

## Q6. What is the biggest limitation right now?

Use this answer:

"The biggest limitation is that this is still a single-table prototype using a public U.S. source. The next stage would be governed validation with local data and a deeper operational model."

## Q7. What would you do next if this continued?

Use this answer:

"We would validate the workflow with governed local Southlake data, add more tailored scenarios, strengthen privacy review, and expand from a single-table prototype into a richer planning model."

## Q8. Who would use this product?

Use this answer:

"Innovation teams, operations planners, analysts, digital strategy leads, and researchers who need safer test data before using governed real-world datasets."
